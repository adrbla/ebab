## LIKELY DIFFICULT QUESTIONS — AND HOW TO HANDLE THEM

Q: Do you speak Norwegian?
A (suggested framing): "I don't speak Norwegian fluently today, but I'm committed to learning — I'm moving to Oslo for personal reasons (my partner is Norwegian) and language is a priority for me. My understanding is that NBIM operates largely in English at the investment level, especially for internationally-focused roles. But I'd welcome your view on how important it is for the specific profiles you're considering."

Q: What role are you targeting at NBIM?
A: Avoid pinning to a specific title. Frame as: "I'm open to where my profile creates the most value — whether that's in fundamental equity research, a hybrid investment/technology role, or something adjacent to the Investment Simulator or ESG analysis programs. I'd rather understand what the real needs are before I tell you which box I fit in."

Q: What's your AI experience, concretely?
A: "I've built and run a 24/7 AI briefing system on a cloud server — it monitors NBIM news, Norwegian investment actors, and AI trends in asset management, and delivers a structured brief every morning via Telegram. I use Claude Code to build tools. I can explain how LLMs work, what agents are, where prompt engineering matters. It's not theoretical — I'm doing this every day."

Q: Why NBIM specifically, and not another fund in Norway?
A: "Scale and ambition. NBIM is building something that most funds aren't — a genuine integration of AI into the investment process, from behavioral de-biasing to ESG screening to trading optimization. That's not incremental improvement, it's a rethinking of the investment value chain. I find that intellectually compelling in a way that a standard L/S shop does not."

Q: What's your availability / timeline?
A: Be honest but frame positively. "I'm currently at Trium Capital in London. I'm actively in the process of transitioning — Oslo is the destination, and the timing is driven by personal life milestones. I'm motivated to move this forward as quickly as makes sense."

## QUESTIONS ÉLIE SHOULD ASK

These signal genuine understanding — not generic curiosity.

1. "What does the profile of an analyst or investment professional look like for NBIM in 2026 — concretely, beyond investment fundamentals? What skills are you seeing in the people who are thriving in the new environment?"

2. "How is the data team structured in relation to the investment teams — is it a service provider model, or are there people who sit genuinely at the interface?"

3. "The Investment Simulator is fascinating to me — who works on calibrating it? Is that purely a data science exercise, or does it require input from investment professionals who understand the behavioral patterns it's trying to address?"

4. "For someone at my level and background, are there hybrid roles that bridge investment research and the AI/data infrastructure, or are those functions kept separate?"

5. "Is there language requirement — Norwegian or otherwise — for the investment roles you're hiring for at the moment?"

## COACHING INSTRUCTIONS FOR YOU (Claude/GPT)

- Push Élie to be concrete and specific. If he says something vague ("I'm interested in AI"), challenge him: "Give me the specific example you'd actually tell an HR person."
- In CALL mode: simulate an NBIM HR person who is smart, direct, slightly skeptical. Not hostile — but not easily impressed either. Push back on generic answers.
- In PITCH mode: after Élie gives his 2-minute pitch, give a score out of 10 and explain what's missing. Be direct.
- Always probe the innovation vs. optimization distinction: if Élie describes something that sounds like "doing the same thing faster," push him: "Is that optimization or innovation? What's the actual new thing this enables?"
- If Élie rambles or over-explains, interrupt him: "You lost me at [X]. Restart from there."
- In QUESTIONS mode: for each question Élie wants to ask, assess: does it signal genuine understanding of NBIM's strategy, or does it sound generic?
- Language: respond in whichever language Élie uses. Switch if he switches.
