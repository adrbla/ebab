# Deep Research Prompt #3 — AI Adoption in Buy-Side Investment Management (Global)

*Calibrated for: Élie Blaise, L/S equity analyst, building AI fluency to differentiate himself professionally. Goal: understand what AI actually looks like in the investment workflow of a fundamental analyst — what's real, what's hype, what matters.*

---

## Context

I am a senior fundamental L/S equity analyst (9+ years in finance, including hedge fund, PE, and IB). I use ChatGPT and Claude daily for research support, but I have not gone deeper. I am building genuine AI fluency — not a superficial understanding, but the kind that holds up when talking to a CIO, a quant, or a technology team at a major asset manager.

My edge as an investor is not data-driven in a narrow sense. I build high-conviction views on businesses through deep research, mosaic thinking, and perception gap identification. I am not a quant. AI for me is a tool to support thinking and judgment — not to replace it.

I want to understand: **what does AI actually look like in the investment workflow of a serious buy-side firm in 2025-2026?** Not the hype, not the press releases — the operational reality. What are fundamental analysts actually doing differently because of AI? Where is it generating real edge? Where does it fall short? And what does "AI-fluent analyst" actually mean in practice?

---

## Research Objective

Produce a comprehensive, sourced report on how AI is being integrated into buy-side investment management globally, with a focus on what this means concretely for fundamental analysts and what constitutes genuine AI fluency at the professional level.

---

## Questions to Answer

### 1. The Real State of AI Adoption (2025-2026)

- What is the actual adoption rate of AI tools among buy-side investment professionals globally? (Separate: hedge funds, long-only, multi-asset, family offices)
- What distinguishes firms that are genuinely integrating AI vs. those that are experimenting superficially?
- What are the most credible industry surveys or studies on AI adoption among institutional investors? What do they find?
- Is there a meaningful gap between what firms say publicly about AI and what they are actually doing?

### 2. AI Tools in the Investment Workflow — What Is Real

For each stage of the investment workflow, describe what AI tools are actually being used, by whom, and with what results:

**Idea generation and screening**
- What AI-powered screening tools exist beyond Bloomberg? (Kensho, AlphaSense, Captide, Alphastream, others)
- Can AI reliably surface under-followed companies or identify narrative dislocations? What are the actual capabilities and limits?
- Are there examples of real investment ideas generated or identified with AI assistance?

**Research and due diligence**
- How are LLMs being used to process earnings transcripts, 10-Ks, industry reports, expert call notes?
- What is the actual time saving vs. depth trade-off? Does AI-assisted research produce shallower or deeper analysis?
- Tools being used: AlphaSense Generative Search, Tegus/Canalyst, Summarize.tech, custom RAG setups — what works in practice?
- What are the documented failure modes (hallucination, missed context, overconfidence)?

**Thesis construction and stress-testing**
- Are analysts using LLMs to structure, challenge, or pressure-test investment theses?
- What prompting approaches (structured frameworks, adversarial prompting, scenario analysis) are proving useful?
- Can AI genuinely help identify blind spots in an investment thesis? What are the limits?

**Monitoring and signal tracking**
- What agentic or automated monitoring workflows are being deployed? (News feeds, earnings call monitoring, regulatory filings, ESG signals)
- What tools support this? (Perplexity, custom agents, AlphaSense alerts, Bloomberg GPT)
- How are portfolio managers using AI to track thesis evolution over time?

**Portfolio construction and risk**
- Is AI touching portfolio construction decisions at fundamental funds, or is this primarily a quant domain?
- What behavioral bias detection tools exist (beyond NBIM's internal tool)?
- Are there examples of AI improving position sizing or exit discipline?

### 3. The AI-Fluent Analyst — What Does It Actually Mean?

- In practical terms, what separates an AI-fluent fundamental analyst from one who is not?
- What specific skills, habits, or capabilities define genuine AI fluency (vs. cosmetic fluency)?
- What is the difference between prompt engineering for research purposes vs. generic chatbot usage?
- Are there specific techniques (RAG, structured prompting, agentic workflows, MCP servers) that are becoming table stakes for investment professionals?
- What does a "personal AI setup" look like for a serious analyst in 2025? What tools, what workflows, what investment in configuration?

### 4. Where AI Generates Real Edge — and Where It Doesn't

- What are the documented cases where AI has contributed to real investment edge (alpha, risk avoidance, operational efficiency)?
- Where does AI consistently fall short in fundamental investment research? What are the structural limitations?
- Is there evidence that AI adoption is compressing or widening the edge of skilled fundamental analysts? (I.e., is AI a threat or an amplifier for someone in my position?)
- What is the honest assessment of AI's ability to replace vs. augment fundamental analysis?

### 5. Emerging Tools and Platforms to Know

- What are the 10-15 AI tools most relevant to a fundamental analyst in 2025? For each: what it does, who uses it, what its actual capabilities are, pricing/access model.
- Which tools are primarily for efficiency (research acceleration) vs. potentially alpha-generating?
- What is the trajectory — which tools are gaining ground, which are being abandoned?
- What is Bloomberg's AI strategy and how does it affect the competitive landscape for third-party tools?

### 6. What Leading Funds Are Doing — Concrete Examples

- Provide detailed examples of how specific funds have integrated AI (Bridgewater AIA Labs, Millennium, Point72, Man Group, Two Sigma, any others with public disclosures)
- What have quant-first funds built that fundamental funds are now adopting?
- Are there examples of smaller funds or boutique managers who have built meaningful AI capabilities? What does that look like at smaller scale?

### 7. The Emerging Professional Norm

- Is AI fluency becoming a hiring requirement at major asset managers? Evidence (job postings, executive statements, industry surveys)
- How are leading asset managers training or upskilling their investment teams on AI?
- What does "AI fluency" look like as a stated or unstated expectation in investment analyst hiring in 2025-2026?
- Are there examples of analysts or PMs who have built visible profiles around AI in investment management? What did they build or publish?

## Output Format

1. **Executive Summary** (500 words max) — the 10 things a fundamental analyst must understand about AI in investment management in 2025
2. **Adoption Landscape** — real state of AI adoption across buy-side segments, with data
3. **Workflow Integration** — section-by-section breakdown of AI in the investment process (idea gen → research → thesis → monitoring → portfolio)
4. **The AI-Fluent Analyst** — concrete definition of what this means in practice, skill by skill
5. **Edge Assessment** — honest analysis of where AI creates real edge vs. hype
6. **Tool Landscape** — structured overview of the 10-15 most relevant tools, with practical assessment
7. **Fund Examples** — concrete case studies of AI integration at named funds
8. **Professional Norms** — hiring expectations, training trends, what the market is pricing in
9. **Implications for a Fundamental Analyst** — specific takeaways for someone with Élie's profile
10. **Source Index** — all sources cited, with URL, publication, and date

---

## Quality Bar

- Prioritize depth over breadth. Better to cover 8 things rigorously than 20 superficially.
- Distinguish between: (a) documented cases with named sources, (b) industry survey data, (c) analyst/journalist inference, (d) vendor claims (treat these with appropriate skepticism).
- Be honest about the hype cycle. If something is more marketing than reality, say so.
- Focus on fundamental/discretionary investment management. Quant hedge funds are a different world — mention them for context but don't let them dominate.
- Avoid generic "AI is transforming finance" framing. Go specific, go concrete, go sourced.
