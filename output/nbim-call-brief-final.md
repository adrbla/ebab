# 4. Key Answers (Expanded)

## 4.1 AI Experience
The main thing I've done is build a daily AI-driven briefing system that tracks topics I care about — including NBIM, developments in AI, and broader asset management trends — and delivers a structured summary each day.

This helps me filter information more efficiently, but more importantly it gives me a clearer and more consistent starting point for thinking about what actually matters.

In my day-to-day work, I also use tools like Claude to help structure investment theses, challenge my assumptions, and map different scenarios. For example, I might use it to stress-test a view or to make sure I'm not missing alternative interpretations of the same data.

What I'm trying to move towards is using AI not just to be faster, but to make my thinking more explicit — particularly around decision-making. That's where I think the real value is.

---

## 4.2 Why NBIM
There are three main reasons why NBIM stands out to me.

First is the scale. The level of access and influence you have as a shareholder is quite unique, and it allows you to engage with companies in a way that most investors simply cannot.

Second is the investment model. The combination of long-term fundamental investing with openness to quantitative tools and new approaches is quite rare. It feels like a place where you can do serious investing while still evolving how the process works.

And third is the direction of travel — particularly how systematically AI is being embedded into the investment process. From what I've seen, the focus is not just on efficiency, but on improving decision-making, which I think is the right way to approach it.

Taken together, it feels like a very compelling environment.

---

## 4.3 Technical / AI Skills Positioning
I would describe myself as non-technical, but very comfortable working with these tools.

I use environments like Cursor to build small tools and automate parts of my workflow with AI assistance. For example, structuring information, creating repeatable workflows, or improving how I organise research inputs.

That said, I don't aim to be a developer. My objective is not to build technology for its own sake, but to design and use systems that make me more effective as an investor.

---

## 4.4 Future of the Analyst Role
I think a lot of the traditional work — gathering and processing information — is becoming increasingly commoditised.

As a result, the edge shifts away from information itself and towards judgment. That means asking better questions, challenging assumptions more effectively, and making better decisions under uncertainty.

In that context, AI doesn't replace the analyst, but it pushes the role to a higher level. It forces you to be clearer in your thinking and more disciplined in how you make decisions.

---

## 4.5 Norway / Language
I would be very comfortable operating in English from day one.

At the same time, I think integrating locally is important, both professionally and personally. So learning Norwegian is definitely a priority for me, and I've already started taking steps in that direction.
