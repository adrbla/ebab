# Vision – EBAB (AI Literacy for Élie Blaise)

## Purpose

Give Élie a solid, credible foundation of AI knowledge and hands-on experience — not cosmetic fluency but real understanding that holds up in professional conversations, interviews, and daily work. The program is grounded in his actual professional context (finance, investment analysis) so that every concept learned connects to something real.

## Audience

- **Élie Blaise** — primary learner and collaborator. 35, L/S equity analyst at Trium Capital (London), 9+ years in finance. Moving to Norway with Emma. Bilingual EN/FR.
- **Adrien B.** — project lead and mentor. Designs the roadmap, prepares research prompts, guides the process.

## Expected outcomes

By April 24, 2026 (hard deadline — Norway trip):

1. **Conversational fluency** — Élie can discuss AI in finance with credibility: what's changing, what matters, what's hype. Not reciting talking points but genuinely understanding.
2. **Hands-on experience** — Élie has set up and used AI tools (agentic workflows, monitoring, research automation). He knows what it feels like to configure and work with these systems.
3. **Showcase artifact(s)** — One or more tangible things Élie can reference or show: a configured agentic setup, a micro-application, a research synthesis. Something that says "I've had my hands in the engine."
4. **A framework for continued learning** — The 4 weeks are a launchpad, not the destination. Élie should leave with the tools and mental models to keep building.

## Objectives

- Map AI's impact on investment management (research pillar)
- Identify key players and their AI strategies — NBIM as starting anchor but broader scope
- Set up personal AI tooling (Open Claw/Patchwork, MCP servers, monitoring workflows)
- Design and build one or more showcase micro-applications (scope TBD)
- Build genuine understanding of core AI concepts through the lens of finance

## Non-objectives

- Becoming a developer or data scientist
- Deep Python proficiency (though basic literacy may emerge naturally)
- Building production-grade applications
- Comprehensive academic coverage of AI/ML theory

## Phasing

This ~100–120 hour block is **Phase 1 — the initial exploration and experience phase**. It is a launchpad, not a destination. The goal is to build enough understanding, hands-on experience, and momentum that Élie can continue growing autonomously afterward, into subsequent phases that will emerge naturally from what he discovers and builds here.

Phase 1 ends with the Norway trip (April 24, 2026). What comes after will be shaped by what Élie learns about himself, the landscape, and the opportunities during this initial immersion.

## Constraints

- **Time (Phase 1)**: ~100–120 hours over 4 weeks (4 weekends full-time + weekday grappillage)
- **Hard deadline (Phase 1)**: Norway trip, week of April 24, 2026
- **Technical starting point**: daily ChatGPT/Claude user, minimal terminal experience
- **Equipment**: Mac Mini with Cursor installed
- **Availability**: weekends (full days), Fridays (often home), some evenings if momentum

## Stage

**Exploration** — transitioning to **Structuring** as the roadmap takes shape.

Stage definitions:
- **Exploration** – Investigating the area. Broad research, gathering sources, forming hypotheses about the right approach.
- **Structuring** – Organizing findings. Defining the learning architecture, key themes, and deliverable structure.
- **Production** – Executing the roadmap. Learning, building, synthesizing. Balance depth and progress.
- **Synthesis** – Finalizing outputs. Cross-checking, polishing showcase artifacts, ensuring coherence.
- **Maintenance** – Post-deadline. Continuing to learn and build on the foundation.
