# **Élie Blaise — Profile**

## **Summary**

Élie is a fundamentally driven investor with strong “craft instincts” — he is energized by deep research, uncovering underfollowed companies, and building differentiated, high-conviction views. His natural edge lies in identifying perception gaps and forming holistic theses on quality businesses, often ahead of the market.

However, his current environment (a catalyst-driven hedge fund) has forced him to operate with shorter time horizons and a greater emphasis on timing, risk/reward, and portfolio construction. This has created a productive tension: Élie respects the importance of catalysts and market dynamics, but feels most aligned with long-term, flexible capital investing (e.g., family office or holding company models).

His primary bottleneck is not idea generation or intelligence, but **decision closure**. He reliably reaches \~70–80% conviction, but struggles in the “last mile” — refining assumptions, committing to positions, and translating views into sizing. This leads to drift, over-analysis, and missed opportunities (often being right, but too early or too small).

The goal of the AI program is therefore not to “teach AI”, but to **design a personal investment operating system** that enhances clarity, accelerates conviction-building, and forces structured decision-making — while preserving his high standards and deep-thinking approach.

Élie’s trajectory reflects a consistent pull toward investing as a craft:

* Started in investment banking (Gleacher Shacklock), then moved to Goldman Sachs in private equity — initially exploring whether to pursue public or private investing

* Gained exposure to multi-asset investing and developed a strong appreciation for flexible mandates  
* Currently at Trium Capital, where he is sharpening portfolio management skills (timing, sizing, risk management) in a catalyst-driven hedge fund environment

His identity is best described not as a “public equities analyst”, but as a **holistic capital allocator**:

* interested in both public and private markets  
* drawn to long-term, unconstrained investment styles  
* motivated by understanding businesses deeply and deploying capital where the opportunity is best

He is particularly aligned with **family office / holding company models**, where:

* time horizon is longer  
* capital deployment is flexible  
* conviction can be expressed without forced exits
