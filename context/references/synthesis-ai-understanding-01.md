## Mental model

### 1. AI is not "learning with you" — it's a tool you brief
- It doesn't improve from your interactions
- It resets every time
- It behaves like a fully trained analyst with no memory

> Output quality = how well you frame the problem

### 2. RAG = unlocking your proprietary mosaic
- The model doesn't know your documents by default
- RAG gives it access to filings, notes, expert calls, internal thinking
- But the real value is: **retrieving the right pieces at the right time**

> This is mosaic thinking at scale

### 3. Real vs cosmetic AI (key distinction)

| NBIM | Others |
|------|--------|
| AI as core infrastructure | AI as marketing / signalling layer |
| Mandatory, top-down | Optional, bottom-up |
| Focused on decision-making, behavioral bias, full integration | Focused on productivity surface |

> Lens: "Who is actually changing how investing works?"

### 4. AI shifts where alpha lives
Like when 10-Ks went online — access edge disappeared, processing edge compressed.

Alpha moves to:
- Interpretation
- Positioning / crowding
- Behavior under pressure
- Conviction

> "We all have the data — the edge is how you act on it"

### 5. NBIM plays a different game
- Not about finding mispriced trades
- Not about speed or information asymmetry

Their edge: **better decisions at massive scale**
- Fewer mistakes
- Better execution
- Behavioral discipline

> Not big alpha trades — millions of slightly better decisions

### 6. The future investor = "conductor"
- You don't do everything manually anymore
- You orchestrate systems

Role becomes:
- Directing analysis
- Deciding what matters
- Arbitrating outputs

> AI amplifies strong fundamental investors

### 7. The core risk
**Productivity without understanding**
- Outsourcing too much
- Losing grip on reasoning
- Becoming a "chooser of outputs"

### 8. Emerging edge
Not data, speed, or processing — but:
> Framing + judgment + conviction under uncertainty

Key skill: knowing where to trust AI vs where to think deeply

---

## Core takeaway (interview-ready)

> "What really struck me is that AI is not just an information tool — it's a shift in how decisions are made. Before, the edge was about accessing and processing information. Now that's getting commoditized. So the role of the analyst is moving up the stack — from doing the work to framing the problem, orchestrating analysis, and making the final call.
>
> What I found interesting is the difference between firms like NBIM, which are treating AI as infrastructure to improve decision-making at scale — including behavioral biases — versus others that are still using it more superficially.
>
> And I think the implication is that alpha doesn't disappear, but it moves. It becomes less about information and more about judgment, positioning, and conviction under uncertainty.
>
> The risk, though, is that as you outsource more to AI, you can lose understanding — so the key skill becomes knowing where to trust the system and where you need to think deeply yourself."

## One-sentence version

> "AI commoditizes information and pushes the analyst's edge toward judgment, framing, and decision-making — but creates a new risk of losing understanding if you outsource too much."
